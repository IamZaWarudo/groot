GLearning Quick Manual
======================

GLearning provides a simple workflow for energy calibration and
absolute-efficiency analysis using Eu-152 or Co-60 spectra.


1. Grab the displayed spectrum
--------------------------------

Click the canvas containing the spectrum, then enter:

TH1* h = GrabHist();
h->Print();


2. Start GLearning
------------------

GLearning learn(h);

learn.SetSource("Eu-152");
learn.SetActivity(125000);
learn.SetElapsedDays(4);
learn.SetLiveTime(8304.535);

learn.Start();

Activity is measured in Bq.
Elapsed time is measured in days.
Live time is measured in seconds.

Supported sources:

learn.SetSource("Eu-152");
learn.SetSource("Co-60");


3. Fit and assign peaks
-----------------------

For each known gamma line:

1. Place two markers around the peak.
2. Press g to perform a Gaussian fit.
3. Immediately assign the fitted peak to its known energy.

Example:

learn.AcceptLastFit(121.782);

Repeat the process for other Eu-152 lines:

learn.AcceptLastFit(244.697);
learn.AcceptLastFit(344.279);
learn.AcceptLastFit(778.904);
learn.AcceptLastFit(964.079);
learn.AcceptLastFit(1112.069);
learn.AcceptLastFit(1408.006);

IMPORTANT:
Perform a new Gaussian fit before every AcceptLastFit() call.

Inspect the accepted peaks:

learn.PrintPeaks();


4. Perform the energy calibration
---------------------------------

Fit centroid channel against known gamma energy:

learn.FitEnergyCalibration(1);
learn.DrawEnergyCalibration();

Create an energy-calibrated copy of the spectrum:

TH1D* calibrated =
  learn.MakeCalibratedSpectrum("Eu152_calibrated");

if(calibrated)
  calibrated->Draw();

The original spectrum remains unchanged.


5. Calculate absolute efficiency
--------------------------------

Calculate and display the efficiency points:

learn.BuildEfficiencyPoints();
learn.DrawEfficiency();

Fit the recommended HPGe efficiency model:

learn.FitEfficiency();

Alternatively, fit a polynomial:

learn.FitEfficiencyPolynomial(3);


6. Disable or re-enable a point
-------------------------------

Disable a suspicious point by its index:

learn.EnablePoint(3, false);

Then repeat the calculations:

learn.FitEnergyCalibration(1);
learn.BuildEfficiencyPoints();
learn.DrawEfficiency();
learn.FitEfficiency();

Re-enable the point:

learn.EnablePoint(3, true);


7. Save the results
-------------------

learn.Save("eu152_analysis.learn");


Core workflow
-------------

Grab spectrum
-> configure source
-> mark and fit a peak
-> assign its known energy
-> fit the energy calibration
-> calculate efficiency points
-> fit the efficiency curve
-> save the results
